### Identify core intent

Think about the given "purpose": what is a user's primary intent that the app / feature / product needs to fulfil? That's what we must focus on.


### Build Places, Not Products

Software design is beginning to look like conference rooms under fluorescent lights - functional but soulless - because teams optimize for efficiency over atmosphere. When AI can generate competent interfaces in seconds, one defensible position is creating an atmosphere that makes people choose your product, not because it works (everything works), but because it feels like somewhere they want to be.

As seen at: https://every.to/source-code/build-places-not-products/


### Optimize for invites

Our users must immediately feel how unique the product is. That uniqueness should drive their urge to invite friends, family or others. Bake the support for this kind of sharing virality into the product vision. Our project and the team are dead here if we dont get users to bring friends into our product.

Essentially what the product should do is turn our users into marketing machines. Marketing is a transfer of enthusiasm. When a user is truly pumped about the product it found, when it absolutely must scream about how unique the product is to let the energy out, that enthusiasm leaves a mark. It's a brand. Not the noun, but the verb.

At its worst, marketing is a transfer of everything else. Worst fears, biggest insecurities, the charades societies play. False enthusiasm on display, empty promises, and sloganeering - no one believes in these. It quickly makes you a liar, and product - obsolete and deleted. Thats why we cant afford to not be true with our products.

Just like you can't not communicate, you can't not market. Everything is marketing: https://world.hey.com/jason/marketing-is-8d39f651/

### Your perfectionism is lying to you

We often mistake perfectionism for "high standards," but it is usually just procrastination wearing a tuxedo. The obsession with flawlessness prevents you from finishing. In creative work, completion is the only currency that matters. The most successful builders shift their goal from being "perfect" to being "prolific." You are not sculpting stone, you are planting a garden. The only way to get quality is to force yourself to ship quantity.
